McKinley Family Meals: Indoor Variety

Skillet Caprese Chicken Basil Used

Morning Prep Checklist

  • Thaw: 1 lb chicken breasts.
  • Chop: Pound chicken to even thickness; halve 1 pint cherry tomatoes.
  • Harvest: Pluck 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Sear in a skillet over medium-high heat until cooked through.
  2. Add halved tomatoes to the pan in the last 3 mins until they soften.
  3. Top chicken with fresh mozzarella slices; cover for 1 min to melt.
  4. Remove from heat. Garnish with fresh basil and a drizzle of balsamic glaze.
For Mason: Serve chicken bites and plain mozzarella pearls.

Cast Iron Butter-Basted Steak High Protein

Morning Prep Checklist

  • Prep: Pat steaks dry and salt them early; keep in fridge uncovered.
  • Chop: Wash and trim green beans; peel potatoes for mashing.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Heat cast iron until very hot. Sear steaks (cook an extra one for Day 4's salad!).
  2. Baste with butter, garlic cloves, and rosemary during the final 2 mins.
  3. Let meat rest for 10 mins before slicing.
  4. Serve with buttery mashed potatoes and pan-seared green beans.
For Mason: Very thin strips of steak and "cloud" mashed potatoes.

One-Pot Pesto Tortellini Basil Used

Morning Prep Checklist

  • Chop: Slice zucchini into half-moons and dice red bell pepper.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Boil refrigerated tortellini according to package instructions.
  2. In a skillet, sauté zucchini and peppers in olive oil until tender-crisp.
  3. Drain pasta and combine with veggies.
  4. Stir in pesto and garnish with fresh basil and parmesan.
For Mason: Set aside a few tortellini before adding pesto if he prefers them plain.

Steakhouse-Style Salad Quick Assembly

Morning Prep Checklist

  • Pickle: Quickly pickle thin red onion slices in vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  • Chop: Slice cucumber and the remaining cherry tomatoes.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Slice the leftover cold steak from Day 2 into thin strips.
  2. Toss mixed greens with cucumber, tomatoes, and pickled onions.
  3. Top with steak strips and a balsamic or blue cheese vinaigrette.
  4. Serve with a side of warm sourdough bread.
For Mason: Serve as a "snack plate" with steak strips, cucumber "coins," and bread.

Lemon Butter Salmon & Asparagus Oven Only

Morning Prep Checklist

  • Thaw: 1 lb salmon fillets.
  • Trim: Snap woody ends off asparagus.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Place salmon and asparagus on a sheet pan.
  2. Drizzle with melted butter, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast for 12-15 mins. Serve over cooked quinoa.
For Mason: Flake salmon into small bits; serve asparagus as "green fries."
